Understanding the Impact of Cars

Every year, around 67 million cars are manufactured globally. Considering all types of vehicles, this number rises to nearly 92 million.

The Environmental Challenge

The transportation sector is a major contributor to CO2 emissions and air pollution. Two new vehicles hit the roads every second, and by 2030, this could increase to more than four new vehicles per second.

Emission Facts and Figures

By 2030, it's estimated that we'll produce around 127 million vehicles globally, contributing significantly to greenhouse gases. About 80-90% of the environmental impact of cars comes from burning fuel and emitting pollutants, causing up to 30,000 premature deaths annually.

Shifting to Sustainable Transportation

We need to move away from fossil fuels to mitigate the environmental impact. Electric cars can cut greenhouse gas emissions in half by 2030 compared to fossil fuel-powered cars, reducing up to 540 megatons of CO2 equivalents.

Fun Fact: That "new car smell" you know? It's actually the smell of phthalates coming from the plastic in the car.
Positive Developments: Recycling and Electrification

Cars are one of the most recycled products globally, with about 85% of all car materials getting reused. In some countries, like Norway, 56% of new cars sold in 2019 were electric.
