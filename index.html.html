Efficiency of EV's in controlling Environmental problems
You know those regular cars that run on Fossil Fuel? Well, they're not great for the environment because they release stuff like greenhouse gases and other yucky things into the air. This can make our planet sick, and it's not so great for our health either. Our project is all about checking out electric cars to see how they can help fix this problem. When we choose electric cars, it's like choosing a cleaner and healthier option for everyone. It's a small change that can make a big difference in the air we all breathe and the world we call home.

About Electric Vehicles

An EV is a shortened acronym for an electric vehicle. EVs are vehicles that are either partially or fully powered on electric power or, EVs for short, are cars that work differently from regular ones. Instead of using fossil fuels like a typical car, they use electricity stored in special batteries or other energy storage devices. This makes them more eco-friendly because they don't pump out any of the smoky stuff that causes air pollution or contributes to climate change. So, they're like the superheroes of the automotive world, helping us have cleaner air and a healthier planet.

Disadvantage of Electric Vehicles

Limited Range

Electric cars can't travel as far on a single charge as regular cars can go with a full tank of gas. So, if you want to go on a long road trip, you might need to plan more and make stops to recharge.

Charging Stations

Sometimes, it's tricky to find places where you can charge your electric car, especially in rural areas. This can be a problem if you don't have a charging point at your home.

Limited Choices

While there are more electric car options available now, there are still fewer choices compared to regular cars. So, you might not find the exact type of electric car you're looking for.


Electric vehicles present certain drawbacks; nevertheless, they offer numerous advantages, such as their positive impact on the environment and the potential for long-term savings on fuel costs.

Understanding the Impact of Cars

Every year, around 67 million cars are manufactured globally. Considering all types of vehicles, this number rises to nearly 92 million.

The Environmental Challenge

The transportation sector is a major contributor to CO2 emissions and air pollution. Two new vehicles hit the roads every second, and by 2030, this could increase to more than four new vehicles per second.

Emission Facts and Figures

By 2030, it's estimated that we'll produce around 127 million vehicles globally, contributing significantly to greenhouse gases. About 80-90% of the environmental impact of cars comes from burning fuel and emitting pollutants, causing up to 30,000 premature deaths annually.

Shifting to Sustainable Transportation

We need to move away from fossil fuels to mitigate the environmental impact. Electric cars can cut greenhouse gas emissions in half by 2030 compared to fossil fuel-powered cars, reducing up to 540 megatons of CO2 equivalents.

Fun Fact: That "new car smell" you know? It's actually the smell of phthalates coming from the plastic in the car.
Positive Developments: Recycling and Electrification

Cars are one of the most recycled products globally, with about 85% of all car materials getting reused. In some countries, like Norway, 56% of new cars sold in 2019 were electric.
